**SBOM Bridging Ontology for Mapping** is a [SKOS](https://www.w3.org/TR/skos-reference/)
vocabulary that provides stable, persistent identifiers for the minimum elements
(baseline attributes) required in a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM), paired with
[SSSOM](https://mapping-commons.github.io/sssom/) crosswalk files that map those
elements to exchange formats.

The vocabulary bridges different SBOM specifications — including the
[NTIA SBOM minimum elements](https://www.ntia.gov/sites/default/files/publications/sbom_minimum_elements_report_0.pdf)
and [G7 AI SBOM](https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2023/10/30/annex-to-the-political-declaration-on-responsible-military-use-of-artificial-intelligence-and-autonomy/) —
and maps them to [SPDX 3.x](https://spdx.github.io/spdx-spec/v3.1/).
