Arthit Suriyawongkul

PhD candidate in AI governance · School of Computer Science and Statistics, Trinity College Dublin

Bangkok, Thailand

Dublin, Ireland

I am an information engineer and researcher working at the intersection of AI governance, open source, and software engineering standards. I am currently a PhD candidate at the School of Computer Science and Statistics, Trinity College Dublin, funded by the Research Ireland Centre for Research Training in Digitally-Enhanced Reality (d-real).

My research asks how software engineering practices and technical standards can bridge the technical and legal layers to make AI systems accountable. A large part of my PhD work is about a vocabulary for system trustworthiness and accountability — mapping the accountability requirements of automated decision-making systems onto the technical capabilities of MLOps.

I am an active contributor to open standards and open-source software. I co-wrote the Linux Foundation whitepaper on the AI Bill of Materials (AI BOM) and contribute to the AI and Dataset profiles of the System Package Data Exchange (SPDX) specification, the Data Privacy Vocabularies and Controls Community Group (DPVCG), and the PyThaiNLP natural language processing library. In 2025 I was a Google Summer of Code contributor adding SPDX 3.0 support to the NTIA conformance checker.

My training spans information technology, cognitive science and natural language, and anthropology — which gives me a cultural lens for translating complex technical systems into policy-ready, auditable practice. I also volunteer for the Thai Netizen Network, a digital civil-rights group based in Bangkok.

    Gopi Krishnan Rajbahadur, Keheliya Gallaba, Elyas Rashno, and 4 more authors
    In Proceedings of the IEEE/ACM 48th International Conference on Software Engineering: Software Engineering in Practice, Apr 2026
    Abs arXiv Bib HTML

    An experience report on developing the AI Bill of Materials (AIBOM) specification, an extension of the ISO/IEC 5962:2021 SPDX SBOM standard that captures AI components such as datasets and iterative training artifacts. Framed through Action Research, it documents a global, multi-stakeholder effort of over 90 contributors, validated against major regulations and ethical standards (EU AI Act, IEEE 7000), industry use cases, practitioner interviews, and an industrial case study.
