2026

  1. ICSE SEIP
    Building an Open AIBOM Standard in the Wild
    Gopi Krishnan Rajbahadur, Keheliya Gallaba, Elyas Rashno, and 4 more authors
    In Proceedings of the IEEE/ACM 48th International Conference on Software Engineering: Software Engineering in Practice, Apr 2026
    Abs arXiv Bib HTML

    An experience report on developing the AI Bill of Materials (AIBOM) specification, an extension of the ISO/IEC 5962:2021 SPDX SBOM standard that captures AI components such as datasets and iterative training artifacts. Framed through Action Research, it documents a global, multi-stakeholder effort of over 90 contributors, validated against major regulations and ethical standards (EU AI Act, IEEE 7000), industry use cases, practitioner interviews, and an industrial case study.

  1. NLP-OSS
    PyThaiNLP: Thai Natural Language Processing in Python
    Wannaphong Phatthiyaphaibun, Korakot Chaovavanich, Charin Polpanumas, and 6 more authors
    In Proceedings of the 3rd Workshop for Natural Language Processing Open Source Software (NLP-OSS 2023), Dec 2023
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

  2. UNU
    Gender-sensitive AI Policy in Southeast Asia
    Eleonore Fournier-Tombs, JeongHyun Lee, Arthit Suriyawongkul, and 8 more authors
    Jan 2023
    Abs Bib HTML

    A multi-country, multi-stakeholder analysis (March–September 2022) across Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia and the Philippines on the state of AI policy, perceived risks to women of AI, and possible policy solutions, with recommendations spanning societal-impact task forces, gender and AI guidelines, regional AI safety standardisation, and investment in women in AI.

  1. NILQ
    Digital Technology and Privacy Attitudes in Times of COVID-19: Formal Legality versus Legal Reality in Ireland
    Edoardo Celeste, Sorcha Montgomery, and Arthit Suriyawongkul
    Northern Ireland Legal Quarterly, Jul 2022
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    A socio-legal interpretation of the SFI-funded PRIVATT empirical study in Ireland, finding shifted privacy attitudes and a greater willingness to share personal data during COVID-19 alongside persistent mistrust of institutions, and arguing that the essence of the rights to privacy and data protection cannot be compressed under EU law.

  1. PRIVATT – Legal Analysis: Digital Technology and Privacy Attitudes in Times of Covid-19: Formal Legality vs Legal Reality in Ireland
    Edoardo Celeste, Sorcha Montgomery, and Arthit Suriyawongkul
    2020
    Abs Bib

    A socio-legal analysis of the PRIVATT empirical study, arguing that people tend to overlook the inalienable essence of the rights to privacy and data protection, and that widespread mistrust evidences a divergence between formal legality and legal reality in Ireland’s COVID-19 technological measures.

    Graham Greenleaf and Arthit Suriyawongkul
    May 2018
